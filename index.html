COMMUNICATION CHANNEL FAILURE PREDICTION IN 5G CELLULAR NETWORKS

Author: Mohammad Ariful Islam

Supervisor: Dr. Israat Haque

Network communications and the Internet shapes our daily activities our daily lives. The 5 GHz radio frequency is the latest addition to the favour of the technology. The 5 GHz link are prone to weather and spatial properties. Natural disasters have potentially huge impact on modern ultra speed 5Ghz radio connections and most of the sensitive operations requires ultra fast communication system which are backed up by the 5 Ghz radio technologies. The radio link failure event occurs due to the spatial and the weather effect on the links. Prediction of such link failure will save the inconvenience and reduce the waste of the financial resources in the high speed radio communication. In this study, we propose an time series based neural network that can learn from historical weather, forecast weather and historical radio link key performance indicators to forecast radio link failure events five days into the future in mobile-backhaul links. Backhaul links will play an important role in establishing 5G telecommunications era and these links are affected by weather conditions. Our proposed approach predict the radio link failure one day ahead by considering rain and wind related the weather events. Also, We compared our approach with existing state-of-art solution in this work. A regional weather forecast data along with the radio tower key performance indicator( KPI) feature data are available which lists expected conditions of the weather data as well as the key features of radio tower sensor respectively. Several steps were taken for processing the weather and the sensor KPI data before training our selected learning model. In this research, we calculated the failure prediction by utilizing the deep learning properties and highlighted the improvements of results in our solution over the existing state-of-the-art solution
